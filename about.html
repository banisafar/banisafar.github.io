Sahar Banisafar

Data Scientist & ML Engineer  ·  New York

I'm a data scientist who came up through mathematics and ended up building production ML systems — real-time bidding models, audience segmentation on terabytes of clickstream data, things that had to work at scale and under pressure.

For six years I worked at Omnicom's data science practice, leading modeling for major pharmaceutical clients. Our RTB optimization system — models served live at an endpoint, scoring bid requests under latency constraints — delivered a 34% reduction in patient acquisition costs. The work was genuinely hard and unforgiving of mistakes.

In 2023 I took time away from full-time work to go deeper on the things I'd been wanting to learn: transformer fine-tuning, RAG architectures, LLM systems. I ran systematic LoRA experiments, built a film similarity system on a 941K-film dataset, and started developing The Balcony — an NYC arthouse cinema listings app. I write about what I'm building here.

Outside of that: I'm a sculptor. A serious cinephile, especially Iranian cinema — Kiarostami, Panahi, the generation that made films under impossible constraints and somehow made them beautiful. I own the domain computervision.art, which tells you something.

I'm drawn to work that sits at the intersection of rigor and taste. The math has to be right and the thing has to be worth building.
